## Brand Basics

### Name
**FlexBook** (not "Flex Book" or "FlexBook App")

### Tagline
"Your trip. Your rules. Your price."

### Tagline Variations (by context)
- **Homepage:** "Where will $40 take you?"
- **Core message:** "Flex cheap flights into epic trips"
- **Simplicity:** "Trip building without the planning"

### Brand Promise
FlexBook empowers spontaneous travelers to discover their next adventure through cheap flights, turning price-first discovery into real, multi-stop trips—frictionless, flexible, and smart.

|-----|------|--------|-------|
| Headlines | Inter Bold or Poppins | 600–700 | Modern, clean, confident |

### Imagery

✅ **DO:**
- Real travelers in real moments (honest, unposed)
- Diverse travelers (solo female, BIPOC, older, families)
- Authentic snapshots (airport corridors, local streets, hostels)
- Show spontaneity and discovery in action

❌ **DON'T:**
- Stock photo perfection
- Overly posed/influencer style
- Clichéd travel imagery (beach sunsets, perfect hotel rooms)
- Sterile or corporate presentation

## Tone of Voice

### Core Tone
**Curious, encouraging, unpretentious.**

Like a knowledgeable friend who travels extensively and celebrates budget travel as *smart*, not sacrificial.

### Voice Pillars

| Pillar | How to Sound |
|--------|--------------|
| **Adventurous** | "Barcelona to Bucharest for $45? That's not just a flight—that's your next chapter." |
| **Conversational** | "Not sure how long to stay? Start with 3 nights and see how you feel." |
| **Authentic** | "You're not roughing it—you're optimizing. And you're going to see amazing things." |
| **Playful** | "Your wallet called. It wants to see three continents this summer." |
| **Empowering** | "This is your trip. Flex it your way." |

### What NOT to Sound Like
- Corporate jargon ("optimize your travel solutions")
- Overly salesy ("EPIC deals!" with multiple exclamation marks)
- Patronizing ("Budget travelers are so brave!")
- Flowery ("Discover the hidden splendors...")
- Fake urgent ("Prices drop in 3 hours!")

### Copy by Context

| Screen/Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Homepage** | Curious, direct | "Where will $40 take you?" |
| **Home screen** | Warm, inviting | "Where are you starting?" |
| **Flight results** | Excited, matter-of-fact | "Cheapest next destinations from Paris" |
| **Empty state** | Candid, helpful | "Crickets today. Try tomorrow—the good stuff usually hides on Tuesday." |
| **Mid-trip decision** | Collaborative, playful | "You're halfway around the world. Keep going?" |
| **Trip complete** | Celebratory, warm | "You just built an epic trip. Ready to lock it in?" |

## Logo & Brand Assets

### Primary Logo
- Full lockup: FlexBook word + icon
- Icon only: For app, favicon, small spaces
- Minimum size: 40px (digital), 15mm (print)
- Clear space: 1x height of "Flex" text

### Colors
- Primary: Teal (#14A085)
- Secondary: White
- Use teal on light backgrounds, white on dark

### Do's & Don'ts
✅ Use provided lockup files
✅ Maintain clear space around logo
✅ Use full color on white/light backgrounds
❌ Don't stretch, skew, or rotate logo
❌ Don't change colors
❌ Don't place on busy/colored backgrounds without white space

## Do's & Don'ts at a Glance

### Content & Messaging
✅ **DO:**
- Celebrate spontaneity and discovery
- Emphasize ease and clarity
- Lead with price as a *feature*
- Build community (share, iterate, collaborate)
- Be conversational and authentic
- Show real people with real experiences

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use sterile industry jargon
- Promise luxury or exclusivity
- Minimize budget travel or apologize for cost
- Create false urgency ("Prices drop soon!")
- Use overly polished, Instagram-perfect imagery
- Sound corporate or corporate-like

### Design & Visual
✅ **DO:**
- Use teal and orange as primary colors
- Choose Inter for body, Poppins for headlines
- Show diverse, real travelers
- Keep layouts simple and clear
- Use white space generously
- Make CTAs clear and action-oriented

❌ **DON'T:**
- Mix more than 2–3 colors
- Use script fonts or decorative typefaces
- Show clichéd travel imagery
- Overcomplicate layouts
- Use inconsistent imagery style
- Change brand colors

## Social Media Voice

### Instagram
- **Vibe:** Inspirational, aspirational, user-generated
- **Hashtags:** #FlexYourTrip #FlexBook #TravelCheap #BudgetTravel
- **Content:** Trip maps, cost breakdowns, user stories, cheap flight finds
- **Example:** "Barcelona → Budapest → Bucharest. Three countries. 10 days. $280. She didn't know she'd end up in Romania when she started. That's the FlexBook way. 🗺️ #FlexYourTrip"

### Twitter/X
- **Vibe:** Direct, snappy, provocative
- **Hashtags:** #FlexYourTrip #TravelHacks #BudgetTravel
- **Content:** Cheap flight alerts, travel facts, tips, responses to trends
- **Example:** "Cheap flights aren't limits. They're opportunities. Flex your trip on FlexBook. Build. Share. Go."

### TikTok
- **Vibe:** Playful, fast-paced, authentic
- **Hashtags:** #FlexYourTrip #FYP #Travel #Budget
- **Content:** Cheap flight reveals, user trip montages, quick tips, trending audio
- **Example POV:** "POV: You just discovered a $45 flight to a country you've never considered. Now what? FlexBook that."

### Reddit
- **Vibe:** Helpful, community-focused, authentic
- **Subreddits:** r/travel, r/backpacking, r/digitalnomad, r/remotework
- **Approach:** Answer questions, share tips, invite feedback (not spammy)
- **Example:** "I built FlexBook because existing trip planning tools felt like work. We'd love your feedback if you try it."

## Copywriting Checklist

Before publishing any FlexBook copy, ask:

- ✅ Would a 25-year-old backpacker say this?
- ✅ Does this celebrate the user's choices?
- ✅ Is this honest about budget travel?
- ✅ Could I say this to a friend?
- ✅ Does this avoid corporate jargon?
- ✅ Is this concise and clear?
- ✅ Does this empower or inspire action?

## Key Brand Differentiators

**Vs. Skyscanner:** We help you build multi-stop trips, not just find flights
